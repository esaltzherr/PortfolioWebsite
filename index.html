Game Projects

Dark Throne

Dark Throne is a 2D platformer Metroidvania where a knight is trapped in a dungeon and must fight and climb their way out, with a primary focus on combat and movement.

My Contributions: Lead programmer and project lead, developed player movement, abilities, backend systems including saving and scene management, and Steam integration.

Extended Game Description: The game features challenging boss fights, intricate level design, and a deep narrative that keeps players engaged. Players unlock new abilities and weapons as they progress, providing a sense of growth and exploration.

Technical Specifications: Built using Unity and C#. Designed a custom save system.

Team and Development Details: Developed over 6 months by a team of 5 for submission to Steam.

Links: Play on Steam | GitHub Repository

Where's My Gun?

This is a wave-survival game where after each wave, you can upgrade an ability. However, the ability you don't choose to upgrade will be disabled for the next wave, so careful balancing is required.

My Contributions: Developed player mechanics, including movement, combat, and abilities.

Extended Game Description: The game features various enemy types, a progression system, and a unique upgrade mechanic that adds strategic depth to each playthrough.

Technical Specifications: Built using JavaScript's Phaser Framework.

Team and Development Details: Developed by a team of 3 over 2 weeks.

Links: Play on Browser | GitHub Repository

Game Jams

Possession Escape (Pizza Jam)

A roguelike game where you are fused with a ghost, trying to escape a haunted mansion. As you progress through floors, you gain upgrades to your ghostly punches or ranged laser. Collect enough souls to free your partner by surviving increasingly challenging scenarios.

My Contributions: Developed all aspects including combat, upgrades, level design, and art (modifying AI-generated art).

Jam Theme: Possessed?

Technical Specifications: Built using Unity and C#.

Team and Development Details: Developed solo in 9 days.

Reflection: As my first game jam, I slightly overscoped, underestimating the time required for art assets. However, it was successful as a prototype. With more polish, such as additional levels and enemy variety, it could become a solid game.

Links: Play on Browser | Pizza Jam's Page
